My Favorite Games

Here are some of my favorite video games of all time. These games stand out to me because of their gameplay, memorability, and the fun experiences I have had with them.

  1. Destiny / Destiny 2

    The Destiny series, initially released in 2014 with its sequel releasing in 2017, is an MMORPG looter-shooter developed by Bungie and published by Sony (previously Activision). It focuses on building up your character by doing various activities to earn gear and experience to level up powerful abilities, and emphasizes experiencing this with other players. It has had a decade-long story and has managed to keep a dedicated community of players. I personally really like the sci-fi setting, the gameplay, and the ability to play missions with and against friends or even completely random people. I have been playing the games and keeping track of the overarching story since the game's initial release in September of 2014.

  2. Metroid Fusion

    Metroid Fusion, released in 2002 (the year I was born!), is an action-adventure game developed and published by Nintendo for the Game Boy Advance. This was the fourth entry into the "Metroid" series, which focused on acquiring different upgrades to unlock new areas of the map and defeat different bosses. Metroid: Fusion is a bit of a departure from the rest of the series, being heavily scripted and mostly linear. This game is one of my favorites mostly due to nostalgia, as it is the first game I ever played. Nevertheless, it is a very fun experience. The base game is well-made and fun, but I think a lot of the fun also comes from the community around the game who popularized things like speedrunning, randomizers that randomly (duh) place upgrades around the map, and even ROM hacks, which modify the game files to potentially provide whole new experiences.

  3. Battlefield 1

    Battlefield 1, released in 2016, is the fifteenth entry into the Battlefield franchise developed by DICE and published by EA. It is a first person shooter that contains a cinematic campaign and a multiplayer that focuses on large-scale battles. This game has been one of my personal favorites since it initially released due to how much care obviously went into its creation. Everything from the weapons to the maps and even the easter eggs were meticulously and precisely crafted. The graphics are beautiful and still hold up even today. I also find the World War 1 setting to be extremely interesting, and a testament to the developers creating such a memorable game in a lesser-known historical period. The game, of course, is fun, which is what matters most.
